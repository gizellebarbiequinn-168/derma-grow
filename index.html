Trend-Alert System

Evidence-informed insights outlining why viral DIY beauty routines can harm, rather than help, your skin barrier structure.

High Risk DIY

Raw Lemon Juice Treatments

Applying raw lemons directly to hyperpigmentation introduces a dangerously low pH levels (~2) and psoralens. Under UV rays, this triggers severe phytophotodermatitis (chemical blistering burns).

💡 Alternative: Look for stable, pH-balanced Niacinamide or Vitamin C formulations.
High Risk DIY

Coarse Baking Soda Scrubs

Baking soda is alkaline (pH ~9), which strips your acid mantle—the skin’s natural lipid defense. Using its rough particles causes microscopic tearing and dehydrates surface layers.

💡 Alternative: Utilize gentle liquid chemical exfoliants (like Salicylic Acid or Lactic Acid).
